First Principles: What Does a Data Center Need?

Data Centers in Space sounds jarring. Let's break it down into first principles.

Ground Inputs

  • Power: Grid interconnection queues are 3-5 years long.
  • Land: Fight zoning, environmental review, community pushback.
  • Water: Water rights are contested and tightening.
  • Labor: Skilled technicians must relocate. Rising costs.

Orbital Inputs

  • Launch: $60k/kg → $1.5k/kg → $10-20/kg (Starship).
  • Manufacturing: Learning curves drive cost down.
  • Power: Near 24/7 solar. No interconnection, no fuel.
  • Cooling: Radiative cooling. No water rights.

The LCOC Framework

Inspired by the energy industry's Levelized Cost of Energy (LCOE), LCOC enables apples-to-apples comparison between radically different architectures.

LCOC = $/delivered GPU-hour with a standard SLA (99.9% uptime), factoring capital expenditure, operating expenses, financing costs, and actual utilization.

Core Equations

Delivered GPU-hours per year:
$$\text{DeliveredGPUh}_y = \text{GPUeq}_y \cdot 8760 \cdot \text{SLA} \cdot \min\left(1,\frac{BW_{\text{avail},y}}{BW_{\text{need},y}}\right) \cdot \min\left(1,\frac{D_y}{C_y}\right)$$
Effective LCOC:
$$\text{LCOC}_{\text{effective},y} = \frac{\text{LCOC}_{\text{base},y}}{u_{\text{sell},y}}$$
Base LCOC:
$$\text{LCOC}_{\text{base}} = \frac{\text{CAPEX} \cdot \text{CRF}(r,n) + \text{OPEX}}{\text{GPUeq} \cdot 8760 \cdot \text{SLA}}$$
Capital Recovery Factor:
$$\text{CRF}(r,n) = \frac{r(1+r)^n}{(1+r)^n - 1}$$

Where:

  • GPUeq = GPU-equivalent compute capacity
  • SLA = 0.999 (99.9% uptime)
  • BWavail / BWneed = Bandwidth sellability
  • Dy / Cy = Demand sellability
  • usell = min(bwSell, demandSell)
  • r = WACC (12% orbital, 8% ground)
  • n = Asset lifetime (years)

Why Now?

Ground compute costs are flattening. The easy sites are taken. Meanwhile, orbital costs are rapidly declining.

I predict crossover sometime in the early 2030s.

If you don't believe in space data centers, you don't believe in compute growth.
